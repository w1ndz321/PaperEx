Organic solar cells, as a cutting-edge sustainable renewable energy technology,
possess a myriad of potential applications, while the bottleneck problem
of less than 20% eﬃciency limits the further development. Simultaneously
achieving an ordered molecular arrangement, appropriate crystalline domain
size, and reduced nonradiative recombination poses a signiﬁcant challenge
and is pivotal for overcoming eﬃciency limitations. This study employs a dual
strategy involving the development of a novel acceptor and ternary blending
to address this challenge. A novel non-fullerene acceptor, SMA, characterized
by a highly ordered arrangement and high lowest unoccupied molecular
orbital energy level, is synthesized. By incorporating SMA as a guest acceptor
in the PM6:BTP-eC9 system, it is observed that SMA staggered the liquid-solid
transition of donor and acceptor, facilitating acceptor crystallization
and ordering while maintaining a suitable domain size. Furthermore, SMA
optimized the vertical morphology and reduced bimolecular recombination.
As a result, the ternary device achieved a champion eﬃciency of 20.22%,
accompanied by increased voltage, short-circuit current density, and ﬁll
factor. Notably, a stabilized eﬃciency of 18.42% is attained for ﬂexible devices.
This study underscores the signiﬁcant potential of a synergistic approach
integrating acceptor material innovation and ternary blending techniques
for optimizing bulk heterojunction morphology and photovoltaic performance.

1. Introduction
Organic solar cells (OSCs), as an inno-
vative power conversion technology, have
garnered increasing attention in recent
decades due to their tunable absorption
and energy levels, lightweight, and semi-
transparency, among other merits[1–8] Since
the invention of the novel non-fullerene
acceptor (NFA) Y6[9] in 2019, OSCs have
witnessed rapid advancements in power
conversion
eﬃciency
(PCE)
in
recent
years.[10–15] The distinctive A-DA’D-A struc-
ture of the Y-series NFA, along with the
utilization of an electron-deﬁcient core,
has led to excellent absorption coeﬃcients
and strong 𝜋-𝜋interactions, providing a
broad and robust absorption window as
well as eﬃcient charge transport.[16–19]
These advancements have enabled OSCs
to achieve PCEs of greater than 18% eas-
ily, with the current maximum reaching
≈19.6%.[20,21] This marks a substantial en-
hancement compared to OSCs employing
the
ITIC-series
acceptor,
which
typi-
cally achieves eﬃciencies of ≈14%.[22–24]
However,
despite
these
improvements,
the photovoltaic performance of existing OSCs still falls short of
that exhibited by silicon, perovskite, and GaAs solar cells, which
can readily achieve PCEs exceeding 25%.[25] This performance
gap can be attributed to the semicrystalline nature and low di-
electric constant of organic conjugated materials.[26]
Upon photoexcitation, excitons (closely bound hole/electron
pairs) are generated instead of free charges. Consequently,
the bulk heterojunction (BHJ) structure is commonly em-
ployed to eﬃciently dissociate excitons by supplying numerous
donor/acceptor interfaces driven by a certain degree of high-
est occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and lowest unoccupied
molecular orbital (LUMO) energy level gaps. Achieving high PCE
performance necessitates a delicate BHJ morphology, which en-
tails nontrivial eﬀorts. First, a balance between the size of crys-
talline phases and amorphous domains must be attained to max-
imize charge transport and exciton dissociation. Additionally, a
highly ordered molecular arrangement is required to facilitate
carrier transport and reduce defects, thereby suppressing charge
recombination to achieve high short-circuit density (JSC) and ﬁll

Despite the existence of numerous methods to optimize mor-
phology, such as the synthesis of new materials,[28] the use
of additives,[21,29] ternary strategies,[30] and ﬁne-tuning fabri-
cation processes,[31] simultaneously optimizing the aforemen-
tioned properties remains a signiﬁcant challenge. In this study,
we employ a combined strategy involving the synthesis of a novel
acceptor and ternary blending to carefully optimize the BHJ mor-
phology. First, a novel NFA, SMA, was designed and synthesized
by extending 𝜋-conjugation along the electron-deﬁcient core of
quinoxaline. The obtained SMA demonstrates highly ordered
molecular arrangement, face-on orientation, and high LUMO en-
ergy levels. Subsequently, a ternary device was constructed using
the host materials PM6:BTP-eC9 and the guest acceptor SMA,
where PM6:BTP-eC9 represents a typical high-eﬃciency system.
Incorporating a small amount of SMA (10%) led to a staggered
liquid-solid transition of the donor and acceptor, thereby facili-
tating the crystallization and ordering of the acceptor. Further-
more, SMA optimized the vertical distribution of exciton gen-
eration, enabling larger crystalline domains, thereby improving
exciton extraction and electron transport and reducing bimolecu-
lar recombination. Consequently, the ternary device of PM6:BTP-
eC9:SMA exhibited a remarkable enhancement in VOC, JSC, and
FF, leading to an improved PCE of 20.22% (certiﬁed 19.66%)
and 18.4% for rigid and ﬂexible devices, respectively, represent-
ing the highest reported values for a single-junction device thus
far. Moreover, the ﬂexible device exhibited impressive mechanical
stability, retaining 96% of its initial eﬃciency after 2000 bending
cycles.

and synthesized based on the A-DA’D-A structure, which has
been widely acknowledged for its eﬃcacy in achieving strong
near-infrared absorption and eﬃcient charge transport. Unlike

intramolecular charge transfer eﬀect, thereby enlarging the
bandgap and enabling higher VOC in the devices.[32,33] Addition-
ally, ﬂuorinated thiophene groups are introduced to the quinox-

termolecular interactions and aggregation tendencies. Moreover,
the presence of extra ﬂuorine atoms potentially induces stronger
van der Waals forces, promoting molecular crystallization.[34] The
detailed synthesis and puriﬁcation process are outlined in the
supporting information. The energy levels of SMA were deter-
mined using the electrochemical cyclic voltammetry method.
As depicted in Figure 1b and Figure S2, Supporting Informa-
tion, the energy levels of HOMO and LUMO are calculated as
−5.59/−4.01 eV (Figure 1b), indicating a higher LUMO energy
level and a larger bandgap of 1.58 eV compared to those of
BTP-eC9. It is widely accepted that a higher LUMO energy level
corresponds to a higher VOC value. Figure 1c displays the ab-
sorption spectra of the host and guest acceptors, BTP-eC9 and
SMA. A blue-shifted absorption peak of 807 nm is observed
for SMA (compared to 828 nm for BTP-eC9), while SMA ex-
hibits a larger Stokes shift of 183 nm (140 nm for BTP-eC9),
as evidenced by photoluminescence (PL) measurements (Figure
S3, Supporting Information). This shift may be attributed to
the reduced backbone rigidity of SMA resulting from rotatable
side thiophene groups.[35,36] Diﬀerential scanning calorimetry
(DSC) was utilized to further evaluate the crystallization prop-
erty (Figure S4, Supporting Information). A clear crystallization
peak was observed corresponding to BTP-eC9 at 301.5 °C, with
an endothermic enthalpy of 31.22 J g−1, indicating good crys-
tallinity. By contrast, SMA exhibited two crystallization peaks
at 236.4 and 278.9 °C, respectively, corresponding to two types
of crystalline phases, indicating its relatively high molecular
ordering.[37] Grazing-incidence wide-angle X-ray scattering (GI-
WAXS) was employed to analyze the packing pattern. As shown
in Figure 1d,e, although both acceptors exhibit a face-on dom-
inated orientation, SMA ﬁlm displays a very narrow diﬀraction
band, indicating highly ordered molecular packing and stronger
crystallinity compared to BTP-eC9. The 1D line-cut along the out-
of-plane direction (Figure 1f) demonstrates that SMA has a larger
𝜋-𝜋stacking distance of 4.05 Å (3.78 Å for BTP-eC9) and a smaller
full width at half maximum (FWHM) of 0.187, corresponding to
a higher crystallinity characterized by a higher coherence length
(CCL) of 30.2 Å (17.7 Å for BTP-eC9). Furthermore, Figure 1g re-
veals the normalized intensity of the (100) diﬀraction signal as a
function of the azimuthal angle. The intensity of SMA decreases
rapidly within the range of 0–25°. Additionally, integrating the in-
tensity of the (100) diﬀraction signal along the azimuthal angle,
the face-on ratio of SMA is as high as 65.1%, considerably higher
than the 41.8% of BTP-eC9. These results indicate that SMA pos-
sesses a highly ordered face-on arrangement.[38]
The photovoltaic performance was assessed by fabricating
devices with the structure of ITO/2PACZ ([2-(9HCarbazol-
9-yl)ethyl]phosphonic acid)/Active layer/PDINN/Ag. In lieu
of
the
commonly
used
poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene)-
poly(styrenesulfonate) (PEDOT:PSS), the self-assembled mono-
layer of 2PACZ served as the hole transport layer due to its
superior transmittance and high work function.[39] Detailed
parameter data and J-V curves are presented in Table 1, Table
S1, Supporting Information, and Figure 2a. The control binary
device of PM6:BTP-eC9 demonstrated a commendable PCE of
19.32% with a VOC of 0.854 V, a JSC of 28.84 mA cm−2, and an
FF of 78.4%. Conversely, PM6:SMA exhibited a notably higher
VOC of 0.920 V; however, the relatively low JSC and FF led to a
lower PCE of 17.91%. However, by incorporating 10% SMA as
the guest acceptor, the ternary device exhibited a substantially
higher PCE of 20.22%, accompanied by an improved VOC of
0.863 V, JSC of 29.10 mA cm−2, and FF of 80.52%, representing
the highest reported value to date. Additionally, an eﬃciency
of 19.66%, obtained from the FUJINAN METROLOGY INSTI-
TUTE, Natural PV Industry Measurement and Testing Center,
is depicted in Figure S5, Supporting Information. The external
quantum eﬃciency (EQE) was subsequently measured to verify
the JSC Value, as illustrated in Figure 2b. The integrated JSC
values of PM6:BTP-eC9, PM6:BTP-eC9:SMA, and PM6:SMA
were calculated as 27.74, 28.06, and 24.49 mA cm−2, respectively,
corresponding well to their measured JSC obtained from the
devices within 5% errors.
Flexible OSCs (f-OSCs) represent promising candidates for
wearable/portable power applications and have garnered signiﬁ-
cant attention. As depicted in Figure 2g, the ﬂexible device based
on a binary blend ﬁlm exhibited a PCE of 17.44% with a VOC of
0.837 V, a JSC of 27.35 mA cm−2, and an FF of 0.762. Upon doping
with 10% SMA, the ternary device achieved an outstanding PCE
of 18.42% with a VOC of 0.859 V, a JSC of 27.81 mA cm−2, and
an FF of 0.771, ranking among the best-performing f-OSCs re-
ported thus far. The enhanced PCE could be attributed to the im-
proved VOC and FF, consistent with the photovoltaic performance
of devices based on glass substrates. Furthermore, the mechan-
ical stability of the device was assessed by applying a bending
radius of 8 mm. After 500 continuous bending cycles, the PCEs
of the f-OSCs maintained 97% of their initial values for ternary
blend-based devices, as shown in Figure 2i. Even after 2000 bend-
ing cycles, the initial eﬃciency was preserved at more than 96%,
validating the excellent mechanical ﬂexibility of ternary f-OSCs.

The evolution of morphology in binary and ternary ﬁlms was
characterized by ex and in situ measurements. First, exciton gen-
eration along the vertical direction of the blend ﬁlms was in-
vestigated using ﬁlm-depth-dependent absorption spectroscopy.
The counter image and exciton generation rate (G) were ob-
tained by simulating the transfer matrix method, as illustrated
in Figure S11, Supporting Information and Figure 2f. The binary
PM6:SMA exhibited a peak G at ≈35 nm, corresponding to its rel-
atively low JSC and FF. Considering that free carriers generated
from excitons must traverse several tens of nanometers before
reaching the electrode, a peak G closer to the center region is
more conducive to charge collection. The peak G of PM6:BTP-
eC9, located at ≈45 nm, aligned with its favorable JSC and FF.
Furthermore, the ternary PM6:BTP-eC9:SMA exhibited a higher
G value and broader distribution in the central region, explaining
its superior JSC and FF parameters.[49]

3. Conclusions
This study designed and synthesized a novel NFA, SMA, charac-
terized by highly ordered arrangement and high LUMO energy
level. By incorporating 10% SMA into the host PM6:BTP-eC9,
the VOC, JSC, and FF of the ternary device were simultaneously
enhanced, resulting in a champion PCE of 20.22%, signiﬁcantly
higher than the 19.3% achieved by the binary PM6:BTP-eC9. The
strong crystallinity and aggregation tendency of SMA facilitated
a staggered S-L transition of donor and acceptor, thereby pro-
moting acceptor ordering in the ternary blend ﬁlm and eﬀec-
tively reducing energetic disorder. Moreover, coupled with the
high LUMO energy level of SMA, the addition of 10% SMA re-
duced nonradiative energy loss from 0.253 to 0.238 eV. Addi-
tionally, SMA optimized the vertical morphology of exciton dis-
tribution, leading to improved exciton extraction. Consequently,
the ternary device exhibited higher VOC, reduced bimolecular re-
combination, and improved charge transport, resulting in su-
perior PCE performance. Furthermore, an outstanding PCE of
18.42% was achieved in ﬂexible OSCs based on the ternary blend
ﬁlm, which retained 96% of the initial PCE after 2000 consecu-
tive bending cycles, demonstrating excellent mechanical stability.
This work underscores the potential of combining the novel ac-
ceptor with ternary blending as a strategy to optimize the BHJ
morphology, thereby paving the way for enhanced photovoltaic
performance.
