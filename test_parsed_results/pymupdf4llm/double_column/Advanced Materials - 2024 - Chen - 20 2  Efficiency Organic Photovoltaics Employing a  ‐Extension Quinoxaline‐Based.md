## **20.2% Efficiency Organic Photovoltaics Employing a** _**𝝅**_ **-Extension Quinoxaline-Based Acceptor with Ordered Arrangement** 

_Zhenyu Chen, Jinfeng Ge,* Wei Song,* Xinyu Tong, Hui Liu, Xueliang Yu, Jing Li, Jingyu Shi, Lin Xie, Chengcheng Han, Quan Liu, and Ziyi Ge*_ 

## **1. Introduction** 

**Organic solar cells, as a cutting-edge sustainable renewable energy technology, possess a myriad of potential applications, while the bottleneck problem of less than 20% efficiency limits the further development. Simultaneously achieving an ordered molecular arrangement, appropriate crystalline domain size, and reduced nonradiative recombination poses a significant challenge and is pivotal for overcoming efficiency limitations. This study employs a dual strategy involving the development of a novel acceptor and ternary blending to address this challenge. A novel non-fullerene acceptor, SMA, characterized by a highly ordered arrangement and high lowest unoccupied molecular orbital energy level, is synthesized. By incorporating SMA as a guest acceptor in the PM6:BTP-eC9 system, it is observed that SMA staggered the liquid-solid transition of donor and acceptor, facilitating acceptor crystallization and ordering while maintaining a suitable domain size. Furthermore, SMA optimized the vertical morphology and reduced bimolecular recombination. As a result, the ternary device achieved a champion efficiency of 20.22%, accompanied by increased voltage, short-circuit current density, and fill factor. Notably, a stabilized efficiency of 18.42% is attained for flexible devices. This study underscores the significant potential of a synergistic approach integrating acceptor material innovation and ternary blending techniques for optimizing bulk heterojunction morphology and photovoltaic performance.** 

Organic solar cells (OSCs), as an innovative power conversion technology, have garnered increasing attention in recent decades due to their tunable absorption and energy levels, lightweight, and semitransparency, among other merits[[][1–8][]] Since the invention of the novel non-fullerene acceptor (NFA) Y6[[][9][]] in 2019, OSCs have witnessed rapid advancements in power conversion efficiency (PCE) in recent **guest acceptoruest acceptorptortor** years.[[][10–15][]] The distinctive A-DA’D-A structure of the Y-series NFA, along with the utilization of an electron-deficient core, has led to excellent absorption coefficients and strong _𝜋_ - _𝜋_ interactions, providing a broad and robust absorption window as well as efficient charge transport.[[][16–19][]] These advancements have enabled OSCs to achieve PCEs of greater than 18% easily, with the current maximum reaching ≈19.6%.[[][20,21][]] This marks a substantial enhancement compared to OSCs employing the ITIC-series acceptor, which typically achieves efficiencies of ≈14%.[[][22–24][]] However, despite these improvements, the photovoltaic performance of existing OSCs still falls short of that exhibited by silicon, perovskite, and GaAs solar cells, which can readily achieve PCEs exceeding 25%.[[][25][]] This performance gap can be attributed to the semicrystalline nature and low dielectric constant of organic conjugated materials.[[][26][]]

Achieving high PCE performance necessitates a delicate BHJ morphology, which entails nontrivial efforts. First, a balance between the size of crystalline phases and amorphous domains must be attained to maximize charge transport and exciton dissociation. Additionally, a highly ordered molecular arrangement is required to facilitate carrier transport and reduce defects, thereby suppressing charge recombination to achieve high short-circuit density ( _J_ SC) and fill

Despite the existence of numerous methods to optimize morphology, such as the synthesis of new materials,[[][28][]] the use of additives,[[][21,29][]] ternary strategies,[[][30][]] and fine-tuning fabrication processes,[[][31][]] simultaneously optimizing the aforementioned properties remains a significant challenge. In this study, we employ a combined strategy involving the synthesis of a novel acceptor and ternary blending to carefully optimize the BHJ morphology. First, a novel NFA, SMA, was designed and synthesized by extending _𝜋_ -conjugation along the electron-deficient core of quinoxaline. The obtained SMA demonstrates highly ordered molecular arrangement, face-on orientation, and high LUMO energy levels. Subsequently, a ternary device was constructed using the host materials PM6:BTP-eC9 and the guest acceptor SMA, where PM6:BTP-eC9 represents a typical high-efficiency system. Incorporating a small amount of SMA (10%) led to a staggered liquid-solid transition of the donor and acceptor, thereby facili- 

tating the crystallization and ordering of the acceptor. Furthermore, SMA optimized the vertical distribution of exciton generation, enabling larger crystalline domains, thereby improving exciton extraction and electron transport and reducing bimolecular recombination. Consequently, the ternary device of PM6:BTPeC9:SMA exhibited a remarkable enhancement in _V_ OC, _J_ SC, and FF, leading to an improved PCE of 20.22% (certified 19.66%) and 18.4% for rigid and flexible devices, respectively, representing the highest reported values for a single-junction device thus far. Moreover, the flexible device exhibited impressive mechanical stability, retaining 96% of its initial efficiency after 2000 bending cycles.

This choice serves to mitigate the intramolecular charge transfer effect, thereby enlarging the bandgap and enabling higher _V_ OC in the devices.[[][32,33][]] Additionally, fluorinated thiophene groups are introduced to the quinoxaline groups. The energy levels of SMA were determined using the electrochemical cyclic voltammetry method. As depicted in Figure 1b and Figure S2, Supporting Information, the energy levels of HOMO and LUMO are calculated as −5.59/−4.01 eV (Figure 1b), indicating a higher LUMO energy level and a larger bandgap of 1.58 eV compared to those of BTP-eC9. It is widely accepted that a higher LUMO energy level corresponds to a higher _V_ OC value. Figure 1c displays the absorption spectra of the host and guest acceptors, BTP-eC9 and SMA. A blue-shifted absorption peak of 807 nm is observed for SMA (compared to 828 nm for BTP-eC9), while SMA exhibits a larger Stokes shift of 183 nm (140 nm for BTP-eC9), as evidenced by photoluminescence (PL) measurements (Figure S3, Supporting Information). This shift may be attributed to the reduced backbone rigidity of SMA resulting from rotatable side thiophene groups.[[][35,36][]] Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) was utilized to further evaluate the crystallization property (Figure S4, Supporting Information). A clear crystallization peak was observed corresponding to BTP-eC9 at 301.5 °C, with an endothermic enthalpy of 31.22 J g[−][1] , indicating good crystallinity. By contrast, SMA exhibited two crystallization peaks at 236.4 and 278.9 °C, respectively, corresponding to two types of crystalline phases, indicating its relatively high molecular ordering.[[][37][]] Grazing-incidence wide-angle X-ray scattering (GIWAXS) was employed to analyze the packing pattern. As shown in Figure 1d,e, although both acceptors exhibit a face-on dominated orientation, SMA film displays a very narrow diffraction band, indicating highly ordered molecular packing and stronger crystallinity compared to BTP-eC9. The 1D line-cut along the outof-plane direction (Figure 1f) demonstrates that SMA has a larger _𝜋_ - _𝜋_ stacking distance of 4.05 Å (3.78 Å for BTP-eC9) and a smaller full width at half maximum (FWHM) of 0.187, corresponding to a higher crystallinity characterized by a higher coherence length (CCL) of 30.2 Å (17.7 Å for BTP-eC9). Furthermore, Figure 1g reveals the normalized intensity of the (100) diffraction signal as a function of the azimuthal angle. The intensity of SMA decreases rapidly within the range of 0–25°. Additionally, integrating the intensity of the (100) diffraction signal along the azimuthal angle, the face-on ratio of SMA is as high as 65.1%, considerably higher than the 41.8% of BTP-eC9. These results indicate that SMA possesses a highly ordered face-on arrangement.[[][38][]] 

The photovoltaic performance was assessed by fabricating devices with the structure of ITO/2PACZ ([2-(9HCarbazol9-yl)ethyl]phosphonic acid)/Active layer/PDINN/Ag. In lieu of the commonly used poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene)poly(styrenesulfonate) (PEDOT:PSS), the self-assembled monolayer of 2PACZ served as the hole transport layer due to its superior transmittance and high work function.[[][39][]] Detailed

The control binary device of PM6:BTP-eC9 demonstrated a commendable PCE of 19.32% with a _V_ OC of 0.854 V, a _J_ SC of 28.84 mA cm[−][2] , and an FF of 78.4%. Conversely, PM6:SMA exhibited a notably higher _V_ OC of 0.920 V; however, the relatively low _J_ SC and FF led to a lower PCE of 17.91%. However, by incorporating 10% SMA as the guest acceptor, the ternary device exhibited a substantially higher PCE of 20.22%, accompanied by an improved _V_ OC of 0.863 V, _J_ SC of 29.10 mA cm[−][2] , and FF of 80.52%, representing the highest reported value to date. Additionally, an efficiency of 19.66%, obtained from the FUJINAN METROLOGY INSTITUTE, Natural PV Industry Measurement and Testing Center, is depicted in Figure S5, Supporting Information. The external quantum efficiency (EQE) was subsequently measured to verify the _J_ SC Value, as illustrated in Figure 2b. The integrated _J_ SC values of PM6:BTP-eC9, PM6:BTP-eC9:SMA, and PM6:SMA were calculated as 27.74, 28.06, and 24.49 mA cm[−][2] , respectively, corresponding well to their measured _J_ SC obtained from the devices within 5% errors. 

Flexible OSCs (f-OSCs) represent promising candidates for wearable/portable power applications and have garnered significant attention. As depicted in Figure 2g, the flexible device based on a binary blend film exhibited a PCE of 17.44% with a _V_ OC of 0.837 V, a _J_ SC of 27.35 mA cm[−][2] , and an FF of 0.762. Upon doping with 10% SMA, the ternary device achieved an outstanding PCE of 18.42% with a _V_ OC of 0.859 V, a _J_ SC of 27.81 mA cm[−][2] , and an FF of 0.771, ranking among the best-performing f-OSCs reported thus far. The enhanced PCE could be attributed to the improved _V_ OC and FF, consistent with the photovoltaic performance of devices based on glass substrates. Furthermore, the mechanical stability of the device was assessed by applying a bending radius of 8 mm. After 500 continuous bending cycles, the PCEs of the f-OSCs maintained 97% of their initial values for ternary blend-based devices, as shown in Figure 2i. Even after 2000 bending cycles, the initial efficiency was preserved at more than 96%, validating the excellent mechanical flexibility of ternary f-OSCs.

The first component, ΔE1, represents radiative recombination loss stemming from absorption above the band gap, an inevitable aspect in solar cells of any type. Both binary and ternary devices exhibited similar values, ≈0.256–0.258 eV, which did not result in any substantial differences in _V_ OC. The evolution of morphology in binary and ternary films was characterized by ex and in situ measurements. In addition to the variation in packing and morphology, the introduction of guest SMA can also influence charge transfer and recombination behavior. Therefore, transient absorption spectroscopy was performed. **Figure 6** a–c displays contour images of binary and ternary films, while Figure 6d,e show the absorption spectra at indicated delay times and decay kinetics at different wavelengths for the ternary film. ## **3. Conclusions** 

This study designed and synthesized a novel NFA, SMA, characterized by highly ordered arrangement and high LUMO energy level. By incorporating 10% SMA into the host PM6:BTP-eC9, the _V_ OC, _J_ SC, and FF of the ternary device were simultaneously enhanced, resulting in a champion PCE of 20.22%, significantly 

higher than the 19.3% achieved by the binary PM6:BTP-eC9. The strong crystallinity and aggregation tendency of SMA facilitated a staggered S-L transition of donor and acceptor, thereby promoting acceptor ordering in the ternary blend film and effectively reducing energetic disorder. Moreover, coupled with the high LUMO energy level of SMA, the addition of 10% SMA reduced nonradiative energy loss from 0.253 to 0.238 eV. Additionally, SMA optimized the vertical morphology of exciton distribution, leading to improved exciton extraction. Consequently, the ternary device exhibited higher _V_ OC, reduced bimolecular recombination, and improved charge transport, resulting in superior PCE performance. Furthermore, an outstanding PCE of 18.42% was achieved in flexible OSCs based on the ternary blend film, which retained 96% of the initial PCE after 2000 consecutive bending cycles, demonstrating excellent mechanical stability. This work underscores the potential of combining the novel acceptor with ternary blending as a strategy to optimize the BHJ morphology, thereby paving the way for enhanced photovoltaic performance.
