2.2.6. Packaging and portability
The increasing demand for rapid on-site testing has placed sig-
nificant requirements on the packaging and portability of LOC devices.
The evolution of packaging and portability has transitioned from two-
dimensional electrode arrays to portable digital microfluidics platforms
integrated with electrochemical sensing, and ultimately to microfluidic
systems that allow droplet manipulation via smartphone controls [127].
Over the past decade, a variety of functional materials, including na-
noparticles, magnetic materials, and conductive composite polymers,
have been progressively employed to enhance the portability of wear-
able systems [132,133]. Zhao, S et al. [134] pioneered an integrated
technology for 3D wafer-level chip-scale packaging. It has a tempera-
ture-controlling system constructed from a high-density serpentine
electrical circuit on the back, minimizing environmental interference
during experiments.
2.2.7. Sensor technology The incorporation of electrochemical detection methods into LOC
systems facilitates cost-effective and highly sensitive molecular detec-
tion (Fig. 5) [135,136]. The selection of electrode materials focuses on
the use of carbon nanomaterials, metal nanoparticles, and conductive
polymers [137–139]. Liu et al. developed an electrochemical sensor
platform for detecting C-reactive protein (CRP) using molecularly im-
printed polymers and doped tungsten disulfide [140]. LOC systems
integrated with biosensors facilitate the direct assessment of physiolo-
gical and biological parameters during cell growth [141,142]. The
combination of aptamers and biomimetic receptors with nano-
technology and LOC technologies enhances the capabilities of biosen-
sing [143].
Furthermore, biosensing platforms integrated into LOC systems de-
monstrate significant potential for nucleic acid extraction, amplifica-
tion, and rapid detection [144,145]. Liu et al. [146,147] synthesized
upconversion nanoparticles as luminescent agents for a luminescence
biosensing platform to detect virus. Asghari et al. [148]summarized the latest developments in optical label-free biosensors, contributing to the
creation of a portable biosensing platform for POCT of viruses char-
acterized by high sensitivity. Sensor technology has also been used for
the detection of tumor biomarkers, Sinibaldi et al. [149] combined
label-free and fluorescent detection modes for the single-step detection
of epidermal growth factor receptor 2 within 20 min, benefiting breast
cancer diagnosis.
2.2.8. Automation and artificial intelligence
In the past decade, the challenge of automating microscale liquid
manipulation has been a significant obstacle hindering the development
of LOC technologies. Durrer et al. developed a robot-assisted system
comprising a robotic arm and an acoustofluidic end effector to achieve
automation [150]. Huang et al. further proposed two complementary
automation processing flows for LOC, designed to improve spatial
layout and enhance time efficiency, termed BigIntegr and FastIntegr
[151].
Artificial intelligence (AI) has the potential to control and enhance
the design of LOC systems, [152,153]enabling rapid analysis of large
datasets, the development of complex statistical models, [154]and the
use of prior experimental results to predict optimization strategies
[155–157]. Moreover, AI has been applied to process cell quantification
analysis in images, yielding results that closely align with conventional
software while demonstrating superior efficiency [158]. The in-
corporation of AI into LOC systems promises significant advantages for
high-throughput detection, novel drug discovery, and POCT [159]. The
evolution of AI and LOC is mutually beneficial, as evidenced by their

4. Discussion
4.1. The application advantages of LOC
LOC devices integrate advanced technologies such as microfluidics,
optical systems, MEMS, and biosensors, enabling precise manipulation
of nanoliter-sized droplets containing biochemical samples and re-
agents. LOC technology consolidates multiple laboratory processes,
including DNA sequencing, RNA sequencing, and biochemical analysis,
onto compact microfluidic platforms. The significant advantages of LOC
devices stem from their capacity for high-throughput and parallel
processing of biological samples, which facilitates rapid data acquisi-
tion and accelerates critical applications such as pathogen screening, drug development, real-time detection, and environmental monitoring
[234].
One of the key strengths of LOC technology lies in its ability to overcome limitations associated with traditional microfluidic systems,
such as low processing throughput and insufficient concentration fac-
tors. Recent advancements have demonstrated the potential of LOC
devices to achieve high-throughput processing while maintaining high efficiency. For example, Xiang et al. developed a multiplex microfluidic
concentrator capable of processing up to 16 milliliters per minute,
significantly enhancing the concentration of cells from large volumes of
biological fluids [235]. This device has proven effective in isolating and
purifying rare malignant tumor cells from substantial volumes of ma-
lignant pleural effusion or peritoneal effusion, thereby providing va-
luable support for clinical decision-making.
In addition to these advancements, LOC technology has been widely
adopted in various fields due to its user-friendliness, portability, and
environmental sustainability. Recent studies have highlighted its ap-
plication in point-of-care diagnostics, where rapid and accurate results
are crucial [236]. For instance, LOC devices have been utilized for real-
time pathogen detection in resource-limited settings, demonstrating
their potential to revolutionize healthcare delivery. Furthermore, the
integration of machine learning algorithms with LOC systems has en-
abled automated data analysis, further enhancing their utility in com-
plex biological assays.
Overall, the continuous evolution of LOC technology has addressed
many of the challenges faced by earlier microfluidic systems, offering a
versatile and efficient platform for a wide range of applications. Future
research should focus on further optimizing these devices for specific
clinical and environmental applications, as well as exploring new ma-
terials and fabrication techniques to enhance their performance and
scalability.
4.2. The prospects of LOC application
4.2.1. Personalized drug screening for tumors
Personalized medicine is an emerging paradigm in healthcare that
tailors diagnosis and treatment strategies to individual genetic profiles,
thereby optimizing disease prevention and management. LOC tech-
nology plays a pivotal role in advancing personalized medicine, parti-
cularly in oncology, by enabling precise and efficient in vitro drug
testing and immunotherapy screening. Unlike traditional methods,
which often rely on trial-and-error approaches, LOC devices integrate
microfluidics, biosensors, and advanced imaging technologies to pro-
vide a miniaturized, high-throughput platform for personalized cancer
treatment.
In the context of cancer therapy, LOC technology addresses critical
limitations of conventional drug screening methods, such as low
throughput, high costs, and lengthy processing times. For example,
patients with advanced malignancies historically endured prolonged
cycles of ineffective chemotherapy before identifying an optimal
treatment regimen. LOC devices, however, facilitate rapid and accurate
drug screening by enabling tumor cell culture, drug uptake analysis,
