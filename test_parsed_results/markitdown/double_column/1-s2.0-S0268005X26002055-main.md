Colorimetric indicators serve as a key technology for the early and sensitive detection of fresh meat spoilage. In
this  study,  a  cellulose/PVA-based  aerogel  with  a  foam-derived  porous  structure  is  prepared  as  a  carrier  for
immobilizing blueberry anthocyanins via non-covalent interactions, forming a pH-responsive indicator (ARCCP-
x). The aerogel maintains a strain loss of 8.8% after 500 compression cycles at 50% strain. Colorimetric tests
demonstrate  that  ARCCP-0.75  produces  a  significant  color  difference  (ΔE  = 32.7  ± 1.88)  even  at  ammonia
concentrations  as  low  as  40  ppm,  while  maintaining  a  stable  response  across  a  wide  humidity  range.  When
applied to monitor pork stored at room temperature, ARCCP-0.75 displays a visible color change (ΔE = 14-16)
during the initial spoilage stage (16-20 h). Moreover, its ΔE shows strong linear correlations with pork TVB-N
content and pH (R > 0.92, p < 0.001). Overall, the aerogel-based indicator provides a simple, rapid, and reli-
able visual approach for monitoring freshness-related changes in meat packaging.

1. Introduction

Monitoring  the  quality  and  safety  of  perishable  products,  such  as
meat and seafood, is a critical challenge in the globalized food supply
chain,  with  significant  implications  for  public  health  and  economic
sustainability (H. Zhang, Noykaew, et al., 2025). According to data from
the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), roughly one-third of the
world's food annually is lost or wasted before reaching consumers, with
a significant proportion of these losses attributed to inadequate storage
conditions  at  the  retail  level  (Bos-Brouwers,  2025;  Xue  et  al.,  2017).
Fresh  meat,  as  a  protein-rich  matrix,  offers  an  ideal  environment  for
microbial  growth.  Under  suboptimal  storage  conditions,  proteins  are
broken down through the combined action of endogenous enzymes and
microorganisms  (such  as  Pseudomonas  and  Enterobacteriaceae),  pro-
ducing  Total  Volatile  Basic  Nitrogen  (TVB-N)—mainly  ammonia,
dimethylamine,  and  trimethylamine  (Gomes  et  al.,  2023;  Shao  et  al.,
2021).  These  metabolites  serve  as  reliable  indicators  of  spoilage  and
exhibit some toxicity (Chang et al., 2019).

Consumers typically use subjective sensory evaluations to assess the
freshness of meat in both retail and domestic environments. However,
by the time visible spoilage or off-odors are detected, the TVB-N content

and total bacterial count in the meat have usually surpassed food safety
limits  (Bekhit  et  al.,  2021;  Tahir  et  al.,  2022).  Moreover,
laboratory-based  detection  methods,  which  involve  destructive  sam-
pling, extended processing times, and complex procedures, are unsuit-
able for real-time monitoring (Cao et al., 2023; Pang et al., 2023; Yanu &
Jakmunee,  2017).  Consequently,  "intelligent  packaging"  technology,
capable  of  providing  a  visual  representation  of  microenvironmental
changes  inside  the  packaging  in  real  time,  has  become  increasingly
critical (Yao et al., 2024).

To  enable  real-time  visualization  of  the  microenvironment  within
food  packaging,  colorimetric  indicators  have  attracted  substantial
research interest in recent years (Luo et al., 2021; Luo et al., 2022; C. Ma
et al., 2025). Their operating principle relies on functional dyes that are
highly  sensitive  either  to  spoilage  metabolites,  such  as  ammonia,
biogenic  amines,  and  hydrogen  sulfide,  or  to  associated  pH  shifts
(Balbinot-Alfaro et al., 2019; Zia et al., 2021). This sensitivity triggers
discernible  color  transitions,  thereby  providing  an  intuitive,  visual
indication  of  food  freshness.  Current  indicator  systems  are  broadly
categorized  into  two  classes:  synthetic  dyes  and  natural  pigments.
Synthetic  dyes,  including  bromothymol  blue  (D.  Yang  et  al.,  2023),
methyl red (P. Lu et al., 2020), and polydiacetylene (S. Kim et al., 2017),

Aerogels represent a class of porous materials distinguished by their
high  porosity  and  extensive  specific  surface  area  (H.  B.  Yang  et  al.,
2024). Within  their interconnected pore networks, both Knudsen and
molecular  diffusion  mechanisms  operate  concurrently  (Szmyt  et  al.,
2021).  This  dual-diffusion  character  endows  the  material  with  a  gas
diffusion coefficient that is three to five orders of magnitude higher than
that of conventional dense polymer membranes. Thus, target gases can
permeate  the  matrix  and  access  active  sites  anchored  on  the  skeletal
framework within seconds. However, conventional inorganic aerogels
such  as  silica  aerogels  fracture  under  compressive  strains  as  low  as
10-20% (M. Liu et al., 2025). Likewise, most synthetic polymeric aero-
gels, including those based on polyimide or polyurethane, exhibit sub-
stantial  permanent  deformation,  with  rates  reaching  60-90%  after
repeated compression (X. Zhang et al., 2020). These inherent mechan-
ical  shortcomings  severely  compromise  their  practical  durability.  In
contrast,  cellulose,  which  is  the  most  abundant  natural  polymer  on
earth,  features  a  high  density  of  hydroxyl  groups  along  its  molecular
chains (X. Lu et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2025; W. Zhang et al. 2023). These
groups not only impart pronounced hydrophilicity and biocompatibility
but also provide abundant chemical sites for covalent grafting of func-
tional  molecules  (Ding,  et  al.,  2023,  2025).  Cellulose  aerogels  can
self-assemble via hydrogen bonding into a resilient, mechanically robust
“skeleton-network” architecture (J. Kim et al., 2025; Zheng et al., 2016).
This  unique  structure  simultaneously  delivers  ultrahigh  gas  perme-
ability and intrinsic sustainability, thereby establishing cellulose aero-
gels  as  an  ideal  carrier  material  for  high-sensitivity  food  freshness
indicator labels.

This  study  successfully  developed  a  non-toxic  and  sustainable  cel-
lulose  aerogel  indicator  label  (ARCCP-x)  for  real-time  monitoring  of
meat  freshness.  Distinct  from  previously  reported  anthocyanin-based
indicators,  this  work  integrated  a  foam-derived  load-bearing  porous
architecture  with  high  cyclic  compressive  resilience,  enabling  rapid
ammonia diffusion at low concentrations while maintaining structural
stability. The internal microstructure and mechanical properties of the
aerogel were systematically characterized. The ΔE of aerogels contain-
ing varying amounts of blueberry anthocyanins was quantified after 30 s
of exposure to 2000 ppm ammonia. The color-responsive behavior of the
aerogel  under  different  humidity  conditions  was  also  evaluated.

2.4. Food freshness monitoring application

3. Results and discussion

Pork samples were placed in a transparent glass Petri dish at room
temperature  together  with  pH-responsive  aerogel  slices  as  freshness
indicators. Images were taken every 8 h. The colorimetric response of
the aerogel was quantified by calculating the ΔE of the L, a, and b values
measured at the beginning and end of the monitoring period.

2.5. pH measurement

At  8-h intervals, three small tissue sections were excised from the
pork samples and individually minced using a meat grinder. The pH of
the resulting meat juice was then measured with a pH probe, and the
mean of the three readings was determined.

2.6. Determination of total volatile basic nitrogen (TVB-N) content

At  8-h intervals, three small tissue sections were excised from the
pork  samples.  These  sections  were  then  analyzed  for  TVB-N  content
using  an  automated  Kjeldahl  nitrogen  analyzer  (Kjeltec  8400,
Denmark). The analysis followed the Chinese national standard method
GB/T 5009.44, and the results from the triplicate measurements were
averaged and expressed in mg/100g.

2.7. Correlation analysis between ΔE and TVB-N and pH changes

To assess the reliability of ΔE as an indicator of pork freshness, linear
regression  analysis  was  employed  to  examine  the  quantitative  re-
lationships between ΔE and both TVB-N and pH. The regression models
were fitted using the ordinary least squares method. All statistical ana-
lyses were conducted with SPSS 26.0.

fibrous network after repeated compression cycles. During loading and
unloading, reversible hydrogen bonding contributes to energy dissipa-
tion and helps maintain the elasticity of the porous structure. This high
resilience  enables  the  aerogel  to  rapidly  restore  its  ordered  porous
framework,  thereby  enhancing  the  synergy  between  molecular  and
Knudsen  diffusion.  Consequently,  efficient  gas  transport  is  preserved,
underpinning  the  long-term  stability  of  the  aerogel's  color-changing
performance.  These  characteristics  ensure  that  the  aerogel  indicator
label  maintains  both  structural  integrity  and  functional  reliability  in
practical food monitoring applications.

3.3. Color response performance of aerogel

Given that the spoilage of fresh meat releases ammonia gas, a series
of pH-responsive aerogels (ARCCP-x) are initially screened using 2000

ppm  ammonia  gas  prior  to  application  testing.  Fig.  5A  shows  color
comparison images of different ARCCP-x samples taken before and after
30 s of exposure, along with their corresponding color difference values
(ΔE). All samples exhibit visible color changes to the naked eye, and the
ΔE generally rises with increasing anthocyanin content. Specifically, the
ΔE is 57.12 for ARCCP-1 and 56.68 for ARCCP-0.75, with a difference of
only  0.44.  According  to  chromaticity  standards,  a  ΔE  above  3.5  is
considered  perceptible. Therefore, at the  perceptual level, there  is no
substantial  difference  in  the  ammonia-induced  color  change  between
ARCCP-0.75 and ARCCP-1. In addition, ARCCP-0.75 maintains a more
balanced combination of strong color response and structural integrity,
whereas  further  increasing  the  anthocyanin  loading  provides  only
marginal improvement in ΔE. Given their comparable performance and
the lower material cost of ARCCP-0.75, this sample is selected for sub-
sequent investigation of pH-responsive behavior. ARCCP-0.75 exhibits a
low bulk density of 0.084 ± 0.003 g/cm3 and a high porosity of (94.32
± 0.21) %, confirming the successful construction of a lightweight three-
dimensional  porous  network  which  reduces  diffusion  resistance  and
enhances  gas  accessibility.  The  pore  size  distribution  (Fig.  S1)  shows
that 76% of the pores fall within the range of 50-200 μm, among which
the 100-150 μm interval accounts for the largest fraction (33%). This
relatively concentrated pore  size distribution indicates a  uniform and
interconnected porous architecture. Such a pore structure, characterized
by  a  high  proportion  of  interconnected  macropores,  is  favorable  for
efficient  ammonia  diffusion  and  contributes  to  the  rapid  colorimetric
response.

To evaluate the sensitivity and detection limit of ARCCP-0.75 toward
ammonia gas, the sample is exposed to atmospheres containing 40 ppm,
400 ppm, 1200 ppm, and 2000 ppm of ammonia gas for 30 s each, and
the  resulting  color  changes  are  recorded.  Fig.  5B  and  C  present  the
colorimetric  responses  of  ARCCP-0.75  before  and  after  exposure  to
different ammonia concentrations, along with the corresponding ΔE. At
a high concentration of 2000 ppm ammonia, the ΔE reaches a maximum
of approximately 55.65. Even at a low concentration of 40 ppm, the ΔE
remains as high as about 32.67. These findings demonstrate that ARCCP-
0.75 exhibits notable sensitivity across a broad range of ammonia gas
concentrations.  The  time-dependent  colorimetric  response  of  ARCCP-
0.75 to NH3 is further analyzed. As shown in Fig. 5D, the ΔE increases
rapidly  at  the  initial  stage  of  exposure  and  gradually  approaches  a
plateau. This rapid early stage increase in ΔE reflects the efficient gas

During  meat  spoilage,  microorganisms  metabolize  nutrients  via

increased active site density. (Alizadeh-Sani et al., 2021; Ameri et al.,

2024; J. Zhang et al., 2019; Zhao et al., 2025). These results suggest that
ARCCP-0.75 can effectively monitor the spoilage process of meat within
a wide range of humidity.

3.4. Application in meat packaging

To  assess  the  response  performance  and  reliability  of  ARCCP-0.75
under  real  food  spoilage  monitoring  conditions,  the  aerogel  samples
are sealed in transparent Petri dishes alongside fresh pork and stored at
room  temperature  (Fig.  6A).  ΔE  of  the  aerogel  is  recorded  at  8-h  in-
tervals, while the pH and total volatile basic nitrogen (TVB-N) content of
the  pork  are  measured  simultaneously.  With  prolonged  storage,  the
white  fatty  portion  of  the  pork  gradually  yellows,  accompanied  by  a
noticeable  color  shift  in  ARCCP-0.75.  After  16  h  of  storage,  a  visible
color change is observed in the aerogel, with ΔE reaching 13.93. The
color variation continues to intensify thereafter, demonstrating that ΔE
can effectively indicate real-time changes in pork freshness.

The mechanism of this coloration behavior stems from the specific
response of the aerogel to the characteristic gases of spoilage. As illus-
trated in the schematic diagram of Fig. 6B, as the protein in pork de-
grades, alkaline volatile gases mainly composed of ammonia (NH3) are
released  and  accumulated  in  a  closed  space.  Upon  contact  with
ammonia, the phenolic hydroxyl groups of the blueberry anthocyanins
loaded  in ARCCP-0.75  undergo deprotonation.  This  structural change
results  in  a  redshift  of  the  maximum  absorption  wavelength,  which
macroscopically corresponds to a color shift from pink to blue or purple.
Initially, the reaction occurs predominantly on the aerogel surface. As
ammonia  concentration  increases,  the  gas  molecules  diffuse  inward
through  the  material's  uniformly  interconnected  porous  network,
reacting  with  additional  anthocyanin  molecules.  This  leads  to  a  pro-
nounced  and  uniform  color  change  throughout  the  material.  Collec-
tively,  these  processes  demonstrate  the  capacity  of  ARCCP-0.75  to
convert imperceptible spoilage gas signals into visually discernible color
changes.

To establish an objective indicator of spoilage progression, the ΔE
was correlated with physicochemical spoilage markers (pH and TVB-N)
(Fig.  6C).  The  Chinese  national  standard  "GB  2707-2016  Food  Safety
National Standard for  Fresh  (Frozen) Livestock  and Poultry Products"
sets the TVB-N limit for fresh pork at ≤15 mg/100g, and the literature
consensus indicates that the initial spoilage stage of fresh meat corre-
sponds to a pH threshold typically above 6.2 (Gonz´alez-Blanco et al.,
2023). When storage time reaches 16-20 h, both TVB-N and pH begin to
exceed these thresholds, indicating that the samples enter the spoilage

4. Conclusion

A  rapid-response  aerogel  colorimetric  indicator  (ARCCP-0.75)
composed  of  blueberry  anthocyanins  and  cellulose  is  successfully
fabricated.  The  material  features  a  well-defined  load-bearing  pore
structure, demonstrating enhanced mechanical resilience and structural
stability. It exhibits a sensitive colorimetric response to ammonia gas,
with  a  maximum  ΔE  of  56.68,  along  with  a  broad  concentration
detection  range  and  favorable  humidity  resistance.  When  applied  in
monitoring  pork  freshness,  the  aerogel  displays  distinct  visual  color
changes  (ΔE  = 14-16)  during  the  initial  stage  of  spoilage  (16-20  h).
Moreover,  the  ΔE  shows  strong  linear  correlations  with  both  TVB-N
content and pH (R > 0.92). These results highlight the material's capa-
bility  to  convert  imperceptible  spoilage  markers  into  visible  signals,
offering  a  promising  approach  for  intelligent  monitoring  packaging.
Future work will further examine long-term stability under prolonged
high-humidity storage to support practical application.
