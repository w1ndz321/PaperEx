1.Introduction
Organic solar cells (OSCs), as an inno-
vative power conversion technology, have
garnered increasing attention in recent
decades due to their tunable absorption
and energy levels, lightweight, and semi-
transparency,amongothermerits [1–8] Since
the invention of the novel non-fullerene
acceptor (NFA) Y6[9] in 2019, OSCs have
witnessed rapid advancements in power
conversion eﬃciency (PCE) in recent
years.[10–15] ThedistinctiveA-DA’D-Astruc-
ture of the Y-series NFA, along with the
utilization of an electron-deﬁcient core,
has led to excellent absorption coeﬃcients
and strong 𝜋-𝜋 interactions, providing a
broad and robust absorption window as
well as eﬃcient charge transport. [16–19]
These advancements have enabled OSCs
to achieve PCEs of greater than 18% eas-
ily, with the current maximum reaching
≈19.6%.[20,21] This marks a substantial en-
hancement compared to OSCs employing
the ITIC-series acceptor, which typi-
cally achieves eﬃciencies of ≈14%.[22–24]
However, despite these improvements,
thephotovoltaicperformanceofexistingOSCsstillfallsshortof
thatexhibitedbysilicon,perovskite,andGaAssolarcells,which
can readily achieve PCEs exceeding 25%.[25] This performance
gap can be attributed to the semicrystalline nature and low di-
electricconstantoforganicconjugatedmaterials. [26]
Upon photoexcitation, excitons (closely bound hole/electron
pairs) are generated instead of free charges. Consequently,
the bulk heterojunction (BHJ) structure is commonly em-
ployed to eﬃciently dissociate excitons by supplying numerous
donor/acceptor interfaces driven by a certain degree of high-
est occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and lowest unoccupied
molecularorbital(LUMO)energylevelgaps.AchievinghighPCE
performancenecessitatesadelicateBHJmorphology,whichen-
tails nontrivial eﬀorts. First, a balance between the size of crys-
tallinephasesandamorphousdomainsmustbeattainedtomax-
imize charge transport and exciton dissociation. Additionally, a
highly ordered molecular arrangement is required to facilitate
carriertransportandreducedefects,therebysuppressingcharge
recombinationtoachievehighshort-circuitdensity( JSC)andﬁll

3.Conclusions
ThisstudydesignedandsynthesizedanovelNFA,SMA,charac-
terized by highly ordered arrangement and high LUMO energy
level. By incorporating 10% SMA into the host PM6:BTP-eC9,
the VOC, JSC, and FF of the ternary device were simultaneously
enhanced,resultinginachampionPCEof20.22%,signiﬁcantly
higherthanthe19.3%achievedbythebinaryPM6:BTP-eC9.The
strong crystallinity and aggregation tendency of SMA facilitated
a staggered S-L transition of donor and acceptor, thereby pro-
moting acceptor ordering in the ternary blend ﬁlm and eﬀec-
tively reducing energetic disorder. Moreover, coupled with the
high LUMO energy level of SMA, the addition of 10% SMA re-
duced nonradiative energy loss from 0.253 to 0.238 eV. Addi-
tionally, SMA optimized the vertical morphology of exciton dis-
tribution, leading to improved exciton extraction. Consequently,
theternarydeviceexhibitedhigher VOC,reducedbimolecularre-
combination, and improved charge transport, resulting in su-
perior PCE performance. Furthermore, an outstanding PCE of
18.42%wasachievedinﬂexibleOSCsbasedontheternaryblend
ﬁlm, which retained 96% of the initial PCE after 2000 consecu-
tivebendingcycles,demonstratingexcellentmechanicalstability.
This work underscores the potential of combining the novel ac-
ceptor with ternary blending as a strategy to optimize the BHJ
morphology, thereby paving the way for enhanced photovoltaic
performance.
